..About Me..

I have been fortunate to find two things I love to do passionately. My life before this, I worked in Pharmacy. I had reached master status in the world of compounding intravenous medications. Antibiotics, Chemotherapy, pain-management for adults, children and babies.

I Worked for a large local hospital in the inpatient pharmacy for more than 12 years. It was there I learned how to handle stress. I was a part of the code team. If a patient in the hospital went into cardiac arrest, they would page us over head. It was our job to rush to the room and take over. At first the stress was off the charts, having the patient’s family standing just out of reach as I compounded lifesaving drugs out of a tackle box on the floor.

I have skills acquired from working 12 years in a large hospital. working as a team in everyday tasks and in high stress moments. As important as working as a team is, I understand the importance of self-motivation. Many of the last years I spent at the hospital where spent on the nightshift, taking care of two hospitals at once. A pharmacist and myself. When I reached my vested level with the hospital, I shifted gears. I worked for a Home Infusion Pharmacy from 2005-2017. I learned how to manage not only my self but others that worked under me.

I was responsible for training new staff, I helped design the Clean Room. Maintained and adhered to USP 797 regulatory standards, maintained and order the pharmaceutical. I handled logistics and compounded all the higher risk drugs. I came to realize that I had reached the top of that life. There was no more challenge, nothing to keep my brain happy. So, I decided to take a step in a different direction

My life has forever been driven by my curiosity. When I find something new I need to know how it works. Even the most unimportant things. I have always been this way, and that’s why I found that I love the world of code. Its like a mixture of Russian nesting dolls and an infinity mirror. No matter how hard you look, you never find the end. This… this is what I love about it. It is a never-ending lifetime of learning. I have learned a lot so far, but I know I have a lot more to learn. I am a carrier changer, so I have a lot of life skills I have learned along the way. As a father I have learned even more. Happiness is a journey not a destination.
